# Section brief template

Every section starts with a 5-line brief. The brief is the contract between you, Stitch, and Nano Banana — all three tools should be working from the same intent.

## The 5 lines

```
Goal:        [What this section must do, in one sentence. One verb.]
Audience:    [The named ICP. As specific as possible.]
Anchor copy: [The headline or key line, verbatim. The line we will NOT change in design.]
Mood:        [Three adjectives. e.g. "editorial, confident, restrained"]
Constraints: [Real data, real imagery, hard rules. e.g. "must include the founder photo"]
```

## Examples

### Hero section for a customer intelligence tool

```
Goal:        Make a $1M+ ARR founder feel seen and curious enough to keep reading.
Audience:    DTC and info-product founders who already have a customer list and an analytics tool that hasn't told them anything they didn't already know.
Anchor copy: 10% of your customers drive half your revenue. You don't know who they are.
Mood:        Editorial, dossier-grade, quietly confident.
Constraints: No SaaS gradients. No stock photo. Must include one real customer name or quote.
```

### Pricing section for a high-touch founding-tier offer

```
Goal:        Frame scarcity without sounding desperate.
Audience:    Marketing operators evaluating a $3,500 + $199/mo commitment.
Anchor copy: 10 founding customers. Then the price doubles.
Mood:        Direct, calm, slightly under-designed.
Constraints: Show the price. Show the number of remaining slots. No urgency banner.
```

### Feature section explaining one product capability

```
Goal:        Make the capability sound like the obvious answer to a problem the reader has lived with.
Audience:    Performance marketers who have been hand-tagging campaigns for years.
Anchor copy: Tag once. Read forever.
Mood:        Technical, tight, no marketing varnish.
Constraints: Include a short code snippet or screenshot showing the actual UI.
```

## Why 5 lines

A brief longer than 5 lines becomes a wishlist. A brief shorter than 5 lines doesn't give Stitch and Nano Banana enough to anchor on. Five is the size that forces one decision per line.

## How to use the brief

1. Paste it into the Stitch prompt as the spec
2. Use the `Mood` and `Audience` lines as the seed for Nano Banana image generation
3. Reference the `Constraints` line during critique to confirm the design didn't drift

The brief is also the commit message. When you ship the section, the first line of the commit is the `Goal` line, verbatim.
